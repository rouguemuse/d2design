Common Questions

New to Paint Care?

What is paint correction and do I need it?
+
Paint correction is the process of removing surface defects like swirl marks, fine scratches, water spots, and haze from your vehicle's clear coat using machine polishers and abrasive compounds. If your paint looks dull or swirled under direct sunlight, you're a great candidate — it's the essential first step before any protective coating.
How long does a ceramic coating last?
+
A professionally applied ceramic coating typically lasts 2–5 years depending on the product tier, environmental exposure, and maintenance. With regular pH-neutral washes and avoiding harsh chemicals, a high-grade coating can last even longer while keeping your paint looking flawless.
How should I wash my car after a ceramic coating?
+
Use a pH-neutral car shampoo with the two-bucket or foam cannon method. Avoid automatic car washes with brushes — they introduce the very swirls you just paid to remove. For the first 7 days after application, avoid washing altogether to let the coating cure. After that, a light maintenance wash every 2–4 weeks keeps it performing at its best.
What's the difference between a detail and paint correction?
+
A detail focuses on cleaning and protecting — washing, decontaminating, waxing or sealing the exterior, and deep cleaning the interior. Paint correction goes further and actually removes physical defects in the clear coat using abrasive compounds. Think of a detail as maintenance and paint correction as restoration. Both can be combined for a complete transformation.
Do you offer mobile detailing or do I come to you?
+
We offer both. Mobile detailing is available for basic to mid-tier services, bringing everything we need to you at home or work. For advanced paint correction and ceramic coating, we prefer a controlled environment at our facility to ensure the highest quality result. Reach out to discuss what works best for your situation.
