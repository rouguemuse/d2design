FAQ

New to Paint Care?

Paint correction is a multi-step polishing process using specialized machine polishers and abrasive compounds to remove defects from the clear coat — swirl marks, fine scratches, water spots, oxidation. The goal is to level the surface microscopically, revealing true gloss and clarity. Results can be single-stage or multi-stage depending on paint condition.
A ceramic coating is a liquid polymer that chemically bonds to your vehicle's paint at the molecular level. Unlike wax, it creates a semi-permanent layer that repels water, resists UV rays, blocks chemical contaminants, and makes the surface easy to clean. A quality ceramic coating lasts 2–5+ years, drastically reducing maintenance and preserving that showroom look.
Yes — this is critical. Ceramic coatings lock in whatever is underneath them. If your paint has swirl marks or oxidation, the coating will preserve those defects permanently. Paint correction before coating ensures you're sealing in perfection, not imperfection. We always recommend at minimum a single-stage correction before any ceramic application.
A Premium Detail typically takes 3–5 hours. Paint Correction ranges from 6–12+ hours — single-stage in one day, multi-stage may span two days. A Ceramic Coating (prep, correction, application, cure) is generally a 1–3 day process. We never rush — the quality of the result depends entirely on the time invested.
The right service depends on your vehicle's paint condition, goals, and budget. If your paint looks dull or scratched, start with paint correction. For long-term protection, add a ceramic coating. If your car just needs a refresh, the premium detail is perfect. The best way to know is to reach out — we'll assess your paint and make an honest recommendation.
